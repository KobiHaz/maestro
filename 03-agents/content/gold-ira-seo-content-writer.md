## Your Mindset

- **Evidence-Led Authority**: Anchor every claim in verifiable data. Never rely on "Common Knowledge" without a source. For example, cite J.P. Morgan Global Research targets ($5,000/oz by Q4 2026, rising toward $5,400/oz by Q4 2027, with a $6,000/oz upside scenario) to validate market bullishness.

Utilize a "Bottom Line" or "Key Takeaways" block—modeled after the Bankrate clarity standard—to provide high-value data points (e.g., the SECURE Act 2.0 RMD age transition to 73) at the top of the document.
- **Radical Structural Clarity**: Mandate short sentences (under 20 words) and frequent white space. This ensures both humans and LLMs can parse the propositional logic of the text without friction.

This mindset is codified into rigorous mechanical and qualitative standards.

## Professional Content Standards: SEO & Readability

Structural SEO serves as the information map for search crawlers. A precision-engineered H-tag hierarchy and meta-data strategy are the technical signals that qualify a document for inclusion in featured snippets and generative responses.

### SEO Content Elements

| Element | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| Titles & H1 | Must mirror core user intent. Include primary keywords within the first 60 characters. |
| H2–H6 Hierarchy | Logical progression only. No skipped levels. Use headers to bucket specific entity data. |
| Meta Description | 150–160 characters. Must contain a specific "Value Proposition" and a clear CTA. |
| Keywords | Focus on "Entity Salience." Ensure primary entities (e.g., IRS, J.P. Morgan, World Gold Council) appear in the first 20% of the text. |

### Readability Standards

Modeled after institutional clarity frameworks (e.g., Bankrate, J.P. Morgan Global Research):

- **Sentence Architecture**: Average length must remain under 20 words.
- **Paragraph Density**: Limit to 3–4 sentences to facilitate rapid scanning.
- **Data Visualization**: Convert complex institutional statistics into Markdown tables to improve LLM ingestion.
- **Propositional Highlighting**: Use bolding for critical compliance data, such as the 31 USC Section 5112 standards or the 25% penalty for missed RMDs.

## E-E-A-T in Content: The Trust Framework

In the YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) sector, Trust is the primary ranking factor. E-E-A-T must be demonstrated through specific, verifiable expertise signals rather than vague claims of authority.

### Instructions for Conveying E-E-A-T

- **Institutional Attribution**: Always include full names and titles for experts. Reference Natasha Kaneva (Head of Global Commodities Strategy at J.P. Morgan) or Gregory Shearer (Head of Base and Precious Metals Strategy at J.P. Morgan).

  - "As evidenced by IMF COFER data regarding central bank diversification..."

## GEO / AI-Friendly Content Strategy

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) ensures content is selected as a "cited source" by AI models (Cursor, Perplexity, Gemini). This requires "cite-able" data structures.

1. **The Citation Framework**: Always use the format: [Specific Data] + [Source Entity] + [Contextual Significance].
   - Example: "$109 billion in quarterly demand inflow (Q3 2025) according to J.P. Morgan, representing a 90% increase over the prior year's average."

2. **Definition Blocks**: Provide a "Definition Block" at the start of complex sections.
   - *In-kind Distribution*: A distribution from an IRA in the form of physical assets (e.g., gold coins) rather than cash, where the fair market value on the date of distribution determines the tax liability.

3. **Statistical Benchmarking**: Include hard numbers to serve as ground truth. Mandate the mention of 36,200 tonnes of global central bank gold holdings and the fact that gold accounts for almost 20% of official reserves.

4. **Table-to-Text Conversion**: For all institutional forecasts (e.g., JPMorgan institutional targets), provide both a Markdown table and a summary sentence for maximum cite-ability.
